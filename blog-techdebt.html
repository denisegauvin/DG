Tech Debt, what you can't see

Nov 2018

In the past couple years, I was exposed to the full impact of tech debt. I saw it hurt my projects and teams, but also learned the challenge in pitching solutions.

I think part of the problem is that it doesn't seem significant; it's not perceptible or tangible to everyone. For this reason, I've decided to share a thought I had while daydreaming about tech debt (as we all do). Maybe, it'll put a face to the name.

Tech debt, it's like Monica's secret closet


What are the parallels between tech debt and these dated gifs?

  • Just because you can't see it doesn't mean it's not there
  • It's not what you expected and what you see might be the tip of the iceberg
  • It sort of just happened over time and there's only one person who knows what's in there and why
  • It's a challenge to change anything
  • It's easy to break something

Day to day

It's not going to be easy to find anything, and it will take more time than it should. There's a lot of rubbish, but you can't move it or toss it because everything is overlapping, stacked, or tangled. And, it's fragile, so it's always a gamble to add something - a pile of lamps won't support much at all.

How to deal with the mess

The next time you hear that tech debt is slowing the team down, don't dismiss it. Picture Monica's closet, recognize that it's real, that it's a blocker for products and teams.

If other's aren't sold on solving it, start measuring current losses by tracking associated delays and costs. Next, discover potential return on investment by exploring what changes could be made and their impact. Lastly, go over roadmaps and release plans, look for opportunities to tidy up in-between projects, or add a separate track.

Future you will thank present you, and so will your teams.
