Dev(Product)Ops

March 2018

It's not often that I refer to myself as a girl in tech vs a person in tech, but I was one of two at a DevOps meetup, and the other was a presenter also named Denise - so I had a laugh and it was memorable. I also learned a lot.

Why the interest in DevOps?

Curiosity. Research to answer the question - what does DevOps have to do with Design and Product Management? And, after conversation and a few good reads, I had a different question - why weren't we all advocates? Where were the product people?

It's product strategy

DevOps is what Product Manager's needs to deliver value with smaller more frequent releases. It creates new opportunities for strategy with big companies, optimizing processes for verification and regulatory reviews. I could bang on about DevOps for medical devices but that's the gist of it

DevOps also offers an in for UX research, with canary releases, pilot studies, and A/B testing for continuous improvement - these are the things a designer dreams of.

As someone who works in the service industry, where research can sometimes be a hard sell, my thought is - let's sell this one thing instead of two (or three).
