AI UX

April 2019

Another meetup, another consolidation of my thoughts and learnings. This one was inspired by Futuremakers talks: Demystifying digital ethics.

AI Ethics

There are definitely gray areas. The exact methods that may allow businesses to profile and target users also overlap with those where ethics come into question.

At the outset, it's the collection of personal data for profiling users. This is an area where controls are already being put in place, and for those of us already designing with GDPR in mind, it's clear that it's not so simple. Then, in the AI, there's a question of the design of the model, as well as potential biases inherent in the data - I reckon we each know at least one horror story.

AI Experience

Don't get me wrong, I do believe there is massive potential for AI and wonderfully customized experiences. What I see, is the need for design. Together, we can identify the controls necessary to avoid narrow profiling and/or noninclusive data, which does have the potential to produce a terrible and prejudiced experience.

Of course, this isn't to say it's easy, it's developing a practice of identifying what you may not have considered in the design of a model as well as the data.

Delivering a more inclusive and therefore ethical experience, somewhat paradoxically may require more data. Data about the users, to be used in the design of the model, to control or introduce bias, and to define the quality and breadth of data used in training.

AI Design

Coming from a UX and psychology background, I approach this from the perspective of the end "user". Drawing from experimental design, we would consider the objective, people and relevant characteristics, and data. Then we would identify assumptions, potential biases, and extraneous variables, and determine what validation and controls were necessary. In the design and application of AI solutions, the same methodology could be applied to control for and/or introduce bias.

Of course, this becomes more complicated when you take an international perspective, or try to consider the end users in a city as multicultural as Toronto. What is an ethical, inclusive, and customized experience then?

I don't yet have an answer

One wild thought I had, was a sort of AI UN - where cultural bias would be assumed in the individual AIs, which would then come together (perhaps to train eachother).

More practically speaking, I've simply strived to consider it more and more in the design of my solutions. At the same time, I continue to advocate for more voices in tech, as I believe diversity will shed light on this as well as other UX issues.
